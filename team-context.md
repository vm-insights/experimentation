## How Personal OKRs Relate to Team OKRs

The team defines **Objectives and Key Results at the team level** each cycle. My personal OKRs should:

1. **Ladder up** — each of my Objectives should clearly map to one or more team-level Objectives.
2. **Contribute to team KRs** — my Key Results should represent my individual contribution toward a team-level Key Result. They are not duplicates of team KRs, but measurable subsets.
3. **Reflect my actual work** — OKRs are generated based on specific tasks I have worked on or am currently working on, grounded in real delivery rather than aspirational targets.
4. **Vary in time horizon** — some tasks complete within a quarter, others span six months or a year. The OKR cycle should match the scope of the work.

## OKR Anti-Patterns to Avoid

<!-- Uncomment or add any that apply based on past feedback from your manager -->

- Do NOT write task-based Key Results like "Ship feature X" or "Deliver dashboard Y" — focus on outcomes.
- Do NOT write vague KRs like "Improve model performance" — always include a measurable target.
- Do NOT create OKRs that don't trace back to a team-level Objective.
- Do NOT set KRs that are entirely within your control (those are tasks, not results) — good KRs have some element of stretch.
